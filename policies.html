Adoption Policies

If you are considering adopting one of our dogs or puppies, please note our policies regarding placement. C.A.R.E takes its responsibility to each dogâ€™s future very seriously. Each animal we serve has come to us seeking a permanent home. In many cases the dogs have been affected by lack of security, consistency, training and basic needs. Our responsibility is to ensure the proper placement of each dog for their future well-being, and our interaction with the dog, our veterinarians, and our policies govern the decisions that we make as a committee.

Our full policies are available here. The policies are summarized below.

Basics

  • We adopt to persons over 21 years of age who own or rent their home, apartment, or condominium.
  • A home visit is required for all adoptions.
  • We ask for, and strongly consider, a veterinary reference. We specifically look for the following:
    • You keep your animals' vaccines and heartworm prevention up-to-date.
    • Your spay or neuter your animals.
    • You maintain your animals' health as recommended.

Fences and Homes

  • We always prefer homes with a constructed fenced yard.
  • We may require a constructed fenced yard for some dogs. The dog's Petfinder page will specify fence requirements.
  • We do not adopt to homes with electric fences. Please read the following articles for more insight into this decision:
    • A true story: Police officer shoots dog behind invisible fence
    • Are electric fences safe and ethical?
  • We require that our dogs be placed in nonsmoking homes.

Puppies

  • We consider our puppies to be dogs of less than 12 months of age.
  • Puppies are adopted only to homes with adults who are home most of each day for socialization and training.
  • We do not place puppies in homes with larger dogs or preschool-aged children for the safety of the puppy and the child.
